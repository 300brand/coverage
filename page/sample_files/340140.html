Waze Launches In-App Advertising Platform

  • By Stephanie Mlot
  • November 7, 2012 05:34pm EST
  • Comments
  • Google 0
  • Share 0
  • Tweet 0
  • Submit 0
  • Share 0
  • Pin It 0
  • Email Print
  • Sponsored By:

Social GPS app Waze is officially launching a new location-guided ad platform for local retailers and nationwide brands.

Aimed at attracting new customers based on identifying potential potential consumers driving in the area near a participating retail outlet, Waze Ads lets merchants to add their business to the crowd-sourcing app.

Waze already claims an audience of 30 million users, boasting average user engagement of more than 440 minutes per month.

The Waze marketing process follows a sort of self-serve platform, allowing businesses to set, change, and measure their campaigns online with a full suite of location-aware tools like local search, advanced targeting based on time of date, and loyalty programs for returning customers.

The new advertising strategy could pay off for businesses, judging by Waze's survey of users, up to 20 percent of whom often call for directions to restaurants, fast food joints, coffee shops, and malls. Those are "huge numbers in terms of potential new customers for a business," Waze said in a statement.

Additionally, more than 50 percent of the app's users said they make pit stops along their normal drive.

During the last few months of testing, Waze Advertising has proved fruitful for companies like Shell, Exxon, Best Buy, Nike, Hertz, and Pizza Hut. The app maker is also looking to add Dunkin' Donuts, Circle K MACS, Kum & Go, Wyndham Hotels, Jamba Juice, and P&G to its marketing lineup.

To celebrate the launch of its advertising system, Waze is offering advertisers, brands, and vendors a $50 free trial of the program during November.

In June, the social GPS company announced its plans to push its app-only navigation system directly into cars, allowing connected vehicles to share information between the smartphone application and their car.

"This is clearly the wave of the future," Waze vice president of platform and publishing, Di-Ann Eisnor, said at the time.

Just last week, Waze updated its iOS and Android apps to include new social features for users, like keeping an eye on where friends are headed and their estimated time of arrival.

The community-based traffic and navigation app is available for free in the Google Play store and Apple App Store.
